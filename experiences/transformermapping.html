TRANSFORMER MAPPING IN FUTA ENVIRONS.

Electricity, is arguably one of the crucial basic amenities needed by humanity. At the Federal University of Technology, Akure(FUTA),students living on the University campus enjoys,to some extent,stable electricity which rumor has it that the University management partnered with the electricity distribution company,well,maybe or maybe not,but the fact remains that the electricity on campus is more stable and reliable than that of the off-campus. So, how then can the students staying off campus get to enjoy this supposed basic amenity? As it’s a very crucial basic amenity for their livelihood. This problem has been identified by Energy Detectors Technologies Limited,a fast growing startup focused on remote data collection, analysis and visualization in the energy sectors with the use of IoT technology and advanced algorithms, are trying in our best capacity to curb this challenge and one of the ways we can is the transformer mapping project we embarked on. Knowing that problem solving projects like this requires accurate field data collection,we partnered with YouthMappersFUTA , a university based student led community that transforms their communities through mapping, in order to also fast track our data collection in record time frame. Geographic Information Technology approach was adopted.
GIS provides a better scenario in the management and monitoring of electricity distribution facilities so as to provide adequate power supply for the resident sector of the economy.
The aim of the project was collect accurate in-situ data of all electricity transformers in FUTA environment by leveraging modern mobile data collection applications that was provided by the company with the purpose of improving the status-quo from a data driven approach. This was carried out by locating and mapping all the facilities involved in distributing energy to the end-users. Further analyses such as determining the trend/pattern of the rate in consumption of energy by the households within the study area would be performed afterwards. Areas of short-comings would also be identified and proposed locations/sites would be generated in the service areas that needs new facilities.

PROJECT OBJECTIVES


  1. Mapping out all electricity transformers delivering power to those living within the environment of FUTA
  3. Estimating the number of individuals/houses feeding from each transformer.
  4. Implementing a real time monitoring system which will remotely aggregate data from all identifies areas feed by different transformers.
  5. Making of some basic analysis which informs both electricity distributors and the users. The objectives were categorized into two phases of which phase one is about data collection on site (1&2) and phase two was the collection of point of contacts information (3&4)

PROJECT TASKS

  1. Acquire point data of all electrical step down transformers within FUTA Environment
  2. Acquire point data of all electrical step down transformers within FUTA Environment
  3. Street / area name where transformers are located
  4. Other neighbouring areas feed by transformer
  5. 3 - 5 locations powered by identified transformer where smart monitoring devices can be deployed. This locations could be either
  • A personal shop/office (e.g small sales shop, hubs, kiosk etc),
  • b) Or public facilities (e.g mosque, church, town hall etc)


HUMAN RESOURCES

During the course of the project, 12 field workers,who are active members of the YouthMappersFUTA community, were deployed on the field and provided with base maps of FUTA and its environs as well as necessary training on how to use the mobile application, in order to collect both the location data and some specified attributes information that were cleared stated in the project description accurately.

SOFTWARE AND HARDWARD USED

  1. GPS Enabled Android Powered Mobile Phones
  2. Point Collector Mobile Application

PROJECT TIMELINE


The first phase of the project was a week long exercise(March 3 rd - March 10th,2020)
The second phase was a week long exercise (March 10th - March 15th,2020)

DATA COLLECTED AND FINDINGS

Although,the data collected on the field by field workers were been synced to the cloud almost immediately after collection,we were still able to find out that there are very few transformers in FUTA environment and this can be connected to the reason why we not only often experience low current of electricity distribution but also days of no electricity.
